# Ethos

## What AnchorStack is not

Not a prompt library.
Not a template collection.
Not a faster way to generate content.

AnchorStack is the methodology for making anchoring part of how you build.

---

## The problem with prompts

Prompts are stateless. Every time you prompt an AI, you start from zero. The decisions you made last week, the boundaries you established last month, the formulations you tested and validated - none of it travels with the output.

This is fine for most tasks. For work where consistency matters, it is not fine.

The drift is invisible. It happens one output at a time.

Week 1: *"Umarise timestamps files using Bitcoin."*
Week 6, after six sessions of prompting without anchors: *"Umarise provides blockchain-verified digital provenance solutions."*

Same product. Unrecognizable language. Nobody decided to change it. It drifted.

By the time you notice, the drift is everywhere - in your docs, your investor deck, your partner emails, your product copy.

---

## The anchor metaphor

An anchor does not make a ship faster. It makes it stay where it should be.

AnchorStack does not make AI faster. It makes AI stay where it should be.

The anchor is not the constraint. The anchor is the knowledge of where the line is - embedded in the process, not in the head of whoever is prompting at that moment.

---

## Why this is different from a linter or style guide

A linter catches syntax. A style guide catches formatting. A CI check catches regressions.

None of them catch semantic drift.

*"Proves you created this file"* passes every linter. It is grammatically correct, stylistically clean, and causes no CI failure. It also overclaims what your product does - and a lawyer will find it.

Anchors operate at the semantic level. They encode what your product means, what it promises, and what it cannot say - and they enforce that meaning across every AI session, every domain, every output.

That is not a linter. That is a compass.

---

## The constitutional question

Every AnchorStack implementation begins with one question - the question your product must never violate. Call it your north star.

For Umarise, that question is:

> *Does this choice make the user dependent on Umarise for the validity of their proof?*

Yours will be different. But the structure is the same: one question you would answer the same way every time, in any context, under any deadline pressure. If the answer is yes - stop. Not "consider it." Stop.

Write this question before you write anything else. It is the most important thing in your guardian.

---

## What an anchor costs

An unconstrained AI output takes 3 seconds to generate and hours to verify it has not drifted.

An anchored output takes 10 seconds to generate and zero time to verify - the constraint already ran.

The cost of an anchor is low. The cost of undetected drift - in a codebase, a product, a legal claim - compounds invisibly until it surfaces at the worst moment.

---

## Solo founders need this first. Teams need it most.

A team has informal anchors: the senior engineer who remembers why that decision was made, the lawyer who reviews copy before it goes out, the designer who catches when something has drifted from the standard.

A solo founder has none of that. Every anchor must be explicit.

But teams scale the problem. Five people prompting the same AI in five different sessions, each starting from zero, each making slightly different decisions - drift compounds with every contributor.

AnchorStack makes the implicit explicit. For solo founders first. For teams at scale.

---

## What AnchorStack is

AnchorStack is the observation that the best time to establish an anchor is before you need it.

The same observation that drives Umarise.

> *At the moment you anchor you do not know if you will ever need it.*
> *But if you need it and you did not anchor - it is too late.*

Anchor it before it matters.
